YELLOWFIN

Description: Yellowfin Tuna, also known as Bigeye Tuna, is a highly prized and commercially important fish species found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide.

Size: Yellowfin Tuna are known for their impressive size. They can reach lengths of up to 8 feet (2.4 meters) and weigh several hundred pounds. The average size, however, is often around 4 to 6 feet (1.2 to 1.8 meters) in length. These powerful and fast-swimming fish are among the larger tuna species.

Color: The coloration of Yellowfin Tuna is characterized by a metallic dark blue to blackish back that transitions to a silver-white belly. They have a yellow or golden strip along their sides, which gives them their common name. The second dorsal fin and the anal fin are both long and bright yellow, another distinctive feature. The pectoral fins are also long and can be falcate (curved), especially in larger individuals.

Features:

  • Tuna Shape
  • Long Pectoral Fins
  • Yellow Tail and Fins
  • Back Stripes
