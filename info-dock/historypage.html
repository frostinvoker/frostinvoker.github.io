HISTORY OF FISHING

Learn about the first fishing methods as and how it ended up becoming the way it is in our modern age.

START READING

500,000 YEARS AGO...

Fish fossils found during archaeological digs have shown us that Homo habilis then Homo erectus were the first fishermen, around 500,000 years ago.

PALEOLITHIC PERIOD

However, fishing probably only really developed after the appearance of Homo sapiens during the Upper Paleolithic period, between 40,000 and 10,000 years BCE. Little is known about the different fishing practices.


Subsistence fishing at that time consisted of catching fish by hand or by using rudimentary tools from natural materials to which no trace remains. This method changed little over the centuries and some techniques are still used today in recreational fishing in the West.

EGYPT 3500 BCE

Fishing in Egypt was depicted in art as a symbol of abundance and fertility. The spear, net, line and rod seem to have appeared almost simultaneously in Egypt around 3500 BCE.

GRAECO-ROMAN PERIOD

Fishing was the main subject of theÂ Halieutika, the earliest surviving treatise on sea fishing written by the poet Oppian of Corycus. The Romans were major consumers and traders of the resources from the Mediterranean Basin.


They fished mainly using different types of nets, and, since the principle of refrigeration has not yet been invented had to ferment fish and transform it into garum, a popular condiment.

MIDDLE AGES - EUROPE

Feudal lords owned the rivers and lakes. River fishing was strictly regulated and permitted for people in religious communities whose diets were punctuated by period of fasting.


However, from the middle of the 11th century, the construction of ponds developed, heralding the beginning of fish farming.


From the 15th century, deep-sea sea fishing and the trade of fish expanded. The Dutch formed fleets of herring drifters which pulled a long drift net and could remain at sea for weeks at a time. They were supplied with provisions by ventjagers (cargo boats) which also brought the catch back to shore

GREAT BRITAIN

Trawlers are fish boats used for catching fish with trawl nets. They first appeared in Great Britain in the 17th century, but the act of trawling expanded rapidly in the 19th century, when sails were replaced by steam power. Boats became bigger and more powerful, and the seafood trade intensified.


During both World Wars, some trawlers were adapted to be used to sweep underwater mines and were armed to protect the fleet of fishermen from enemy vessels

18TH CENTURY

Fly fishing is the method of fishing with a fishing rod that we all know of today. It started in the 18th century and it was initially reserved for the wealthy classes. It gradually became more accessible as technological advances meant better equipment could be produced relatively cheaply.
